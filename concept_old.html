01

The Core Vision & Problem Statement

The Enterprise Paradox Today

Global enterprises running Drupal face a structural contradiction: centrally managed design systems provide consistency but throttle creativity. Local site owners need flexibility to differentiate, but unbounded freedom destroys brand cohesion and creates accessibility, security, and maintenance debt.

Meanwhile, AI design tools are generating extraordinary visual output but exist outside the governed CMS environment — resulting in disconnected one-offs that bypass design system rules.

The answer is not choosing between control and freedom. It is redesigning the constraint surface so that AI-powered creativity operates within structurally enforced quality gates.

The Target State in One Sentence

An AI-augmented, token-driven, component-first Drupal platform where any authorized editor can compose beautiful, WCAG AA–compliant, brand-coherent websites — and where the system structurally prevents the creation of anything insecure, inaccessible, or off-brand.

```

Three Structural Guarantees

Quality by Construction: Components are pre-built, pre-tested, and pre-validated. Editors assemble — they do not build from scratch.

AI as a Constrained Composer: AI generates, selects, and arranges from the design system's vocabulary. It does not escape it.

Governance Without Bureaucracy: Rules are code, not committees. Approval workflows trigger only for exceptions outside the system's validated envelope.

```

The design system is not a style guide PDF. It is a living, versioned, machine-readable contract between designers, developers, content authors, and AI — enforced at build time, deploy time, and render time.

03

AI-Powered Design at Enterprise Scale

The AI layer is not a free-form design generator. It is a constrained intelligence that knows the full vocabulary of the design system and composes within it — enabling unprecedented speed and creativity without sacrificing governance.

AI Design Prompter

Site owners describe their page intent in natural language: "A product landing page for a B2B SaaS tool aimed at enterprise security teams, authoritative but approachable."

The AI returns 3–5 layout proposals, each using only approved components, tokens, and patterns from the design system. Editors choose, customize within bounds, and publish.

Token-Aware Style Variance

AI can propose expressive visual variants — a dark hero section, an editorial image-lead layout, a data-heavy dashboard — all derived from the same token set. The brand is consistent; the expression is flexible.

Each variant is validated against contrast ratios and component constraints before being offered to the editor.

Content Intelligence

AI auto-generates alt text, meta descriptions, structured data markup, and content summaries from page content. Tone-of-voice rules are encoded as system prompts per brand.

Readability scores, inclusive language checks, and SEO signals are surfaced in the authoring UI, not in a post-hoc audit.

Component Evolution Loop

AI monitors usage patterns and performance data across all sites. It surfaces proposals for new component variants based on what editors are trying to build outside the system.

The design system team reviews proposals, builds and validates them, and ships them to the registry — closing the creativity gap systematically.

Automated Accessibility Audit

Every AI-generated layout is run through axe-core, Pa11y, and a custom WCAG AA checker before being presented to editors. Layouts with violations are either auto-corrected or marked with mandatory fixes.

Editors cannot accidentally ship inaccessible pages; the system catches violations before they reach production.

Security Intelligence

AI-generated layouts are scanned for CSP violations, XSS surface areas, and insecure third-party embeds. Permitted external resources are whitelisted at the platform level; AI cannot introduce unapproved dependencies.

Dependency graphs are auto-updated and security advisories trigger automated component patches across all sites.

```
Audit, Unify & Build the Design Foundation

The goal of Phase 1 is to stop the bleeding: eliminate design drift, establish the canonical token system, migrate the existing Drupal component landscape to SDC, and put automated quality gates in place. No site owner-facing AI features yet — this phase builds the infrastructure they depend on.

Quality Gate Infrastructure: Deploy automated WCAG AA, performance (Core Web Vitals), and OWASP scanning into the Drupal deployment pipeline. Define pass/fail thresholds. Implement security headers, CSP policies, and RBAC audit logging across all instances.

Global Rollout, Self-Service & Continuous Intelligence

The goal of Phase 3 is to make the platform the default way all enterprise web properties are built and operated. Full global rollout, self-service site provisioning, proactive AI-driven quality management, and a platform that continuously improves itself based on performance and usage data.

Key Activities

Self-Service Site Provisioning: Authorized teams can spin up a new Drupal site instance via a GitOps workflow in under 30 minutes. The site arrives pre-configured with the correct brand token set, component library version, security baseline, quality gates, and CI/CD pipeline. No central team involvement required for provisioning.

Proactive Quality Intelligence: Move from reactive to proactive quality management. AI monitors all live sites continuously, flags regressions in accessibility, performance, or security, and auto-generates remediation PRs where possible. The governance cockpit becomes a live health system rather than a periodic audit tool.

AI-Driven A/B Testing: The platform can autonomously propose and run layout experiments based on analytics signals. AI proposes variant layouts (within design system bounds), A/B tests them, and surfaces winners for editor approval. Continuous conversion and engagement optimization at scale.

Design System as a Living Standard: Establish a formal RFC (Request for Change) process where any team globally can propose component additions, token changes, or pattern updates. AI assists in evaluating proposals for consistency, accessibility, and duplication. Platform team reviews quarterly; approved changes ship to all sites automatically.

Federated Governance Model: Central platform team maintains the foundation layer and token system. Regional/brand platform teams maintain their token overlays. Site teams operate with full self-service within their scope. Escalation paths are clear; autonomy is maximized at each level without sacrificing global standards.
```

Global Operating Model

The Federated Platform Team Structure

Global enterprise web platforms fail when they try to centralize everything. This model uses a federated ownership model with clear scope at each level.

Central Platform Team (10–15 people globally): Owns the Drupal core platform, the token system foundation, the CI/CD infrastructure, security baseline, and the component registry governance process. Operates as an internal product team with a public roadmap.

Regional / Brand Platform Teams (3–5 people per brand): Own their token overlay set, regional component variants, and regional deployment pipelines. Contribute to the central registry via the RFC process. First-line support for site teams in their brand.

Site Teams (1–3 people per site): Own their site's content, layout decisions within bounds, and stakeholder relationships. Consume platform capabilities as a self-service product. No Drupal core or security responsibility.

The Governance Cockpit

The central governance dashboard provides real-time visibility across the entire global fleet without requiring manual reporting from site teams.

Tracked automatically: Component adoption rates by version, token override inventory, WCAG AA compliance score per site, Core Web Vitals P75 per region, security advisory status, Drupal core/module version currency, and AI composition usage patterns.

Escalation logic: Sites with <90% WCAG compliance, critical security advisories unpatched >7 days, or core web vitals failing for >14 days trigger automatic escalations to the regional platform team. The platform team does not need to manually monitor hundreds of sites.

```

Design System Versioning & Deployment

The component registry uses semantic versioning with automated compatibility checks. Sites pin to a minor version; the platform team auto-deploys patch releases. Minor version upgrades include a compatibility report generated by AI before deployment. Major version changes include a migration assistant.
```

Critical Success Factors

Design System Discipline

The entire architecture collapses if the design token system is not maintained with engineering-grade rigor. Treat it as a product, not a project. Assign clear ownership, versioning, and a public roadmap. Never allow components to ship without token compliance.

AI as Assistant, Not Autopilot

AI generates proposals; humans approve them. The system's value comes from dramatically reducing the cost of good decisions, not from removing human judgment. Editors who understand why the system works will be better collaborators with the AI than those who simply click "accept."

Federated Community Building

The platform's long-term quality depends on site teams feeling like contributors, not just consumers. Invest in the RFC process, run quarterly design system community calls, celebrate component contributions, and make the feedback loop visible. Governance that feels participatory sustains adoption.

Incremental Migration

Do not attempt a big-bang migration of all sites. Use a strangler fig pattern: new pages use new components, old pages migrate on next major update. Measure adoption by component coverage, not site count. Progress should be visible within weeks of Phase 1 launch.

Security as Default, Not Add-On

Security requirements must be baked into the component contract from day one. A component that fails security review does not enter the registry. Retrofitting security into a live fleet of components is orders of magnitude more expensive than requiring it upfront.

Measure What Matters

Track time-to-publish, a11y regression rate, component reuse ratio, and editor satisfaction — not lines of code or component count. The platform's value is measured in editor productivity and quality outcomes, not engineering output. Make these metrics visible to all stakeholders from day one.

This is not a technology project. It is a product strategy that uses technology to give every web team in the enterprise the creative power of a design agency and the quality discipline of a platform engineering team — simultaneously, at global scale.
